About Us

What We Do

Mind Your Head is an Oxford University based organisation which aims to raise awareness and reduce the stigma of mental health issues.

One in four people will have mental health problems at some point in their lives. Yet, according to a Time to Change survey, 60% of those surveyed who suffer from a mental health condition say that the stigma surrounding their condition is as bad as, or worse than, the condition itself. As a society, we are largely silent about mental health, associating it with weakness, unpredictability or danger.

Mind Your Head is designed to get people thinking and talking about mental health and wellbeing. Even in a progressive community like Oxford, mental health is an awkward and poorly-understood subject, but there is no reason that it should be like that. We want to create a more welcoming culture for people who have experienced mental health problems, to spread the word about what ‘mental illness’ really means, and to encourage every student to look after their mental wellbeing.

Achievements

Mind You Head is proud to announce that over the last year the campaign has gone from strength to strength. We have:

Continued to host college awareness events.

Increased the number and variety of testimonials on the website.

Teamed up with Student Minds to host a successful Christmas fundraiser and awareness event.

Developed, and gained support for, the new It Gets Brighter Project, including: Working with a web development team in Toronto to develop a website with students at Stanford; gaining support and videos from celebrities and eminent clinicians; developing an international reach; working to produce a high quality promotional video to launch the project.

Produced surveys to gather crucial welfare information from different colleges, in order to improve and ensure universal standards of welfare provision across the university.

Re-structured our team to allocate our priorities to dedicated project leaders.

Organised a team of individuals who are available to talk openly about their experiences with mental health at awareness events.

Created an extensive online directory of resources for coping with mental health and related issues.

Worked to redevelop the website and social media.

Raised awareness through the naked OUWRFC calendar which raised over £3000 for Mind Your Head.

Worked alongside the Oxford Sports Federation to develop guidelines of ways to support students in university-level sport.

Planned an exciting and helpful Wellbeing Week: Coming soon!

Who We Are

Joshua Chauvin

Hello, I'm Joshua Chauvin, a DPhil student in Experimental Psychology at New College, and I chair the Mind Your Head campaign alongside Sophie. I have a longstanding commitment to mental health issues and to de-stigmatization and awareness efforts. Before I became an executive member of Mind Your Head Oxford in 2012, I worked as a research consultant at the Canadian Mental Health Association, and have created two short documentaries combatting stigmatization surrounding mental illness as well as HIV/AIDS. Even with a long history in working with and leading student organizations, I’ve never been prouder or more inspired than I have been working with the people who populate this page — and those that have gone unmentioned. Without their passion and commitment, this website as well as the many events that we put on would not be possible.

To those among you that find yourself struggling with a mental illness, as well as those of you that support someone with a mental illness, we hope that you will find what we do beneficial.
